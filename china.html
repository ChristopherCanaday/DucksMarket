Fiesta China

      Fiesta dinnerware was first produced in Newell, West Virginia in 1936 by Homer Laughlin China and continued production until 1973. It was introduced again in 1986. Fiesta China is best known for its art deco and concentric circles. From the beginning it has been a collectible. According to Stephanie Duck, "Almost everyone can find a color or two or eight that they love." In 2002, The New York Times call Fiesta "the most collected brand of china in the United States." Our Fiesta dinnerware is sold open stock. We have a wide variety of colors, basic pieces, and one of the largest selections of serving pieces you will ever see. Prices start at $3.98.
